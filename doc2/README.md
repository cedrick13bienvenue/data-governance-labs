## What This Document Contains

A Compliance Response Matrix analysing three simultaneous deletion requests from customers in different locations, all assessed under Rwanda Law No. 058/2021.

### Customer A — Abena (Accra, Ghana)
- **Decision:** Approve deletion in full
- **Retention exception:** Financial transaction records under Rwandan financial regulations
- **Deadline:** 30 days (no statutory deadline in Rwanda DPL — 30 days is best practice)
- **Key articles:** Art. 7, 8, 13-14, 38

### Customer B — Lukas (Berlin, Germany)
- **Decision:** Approve deletion in full
- **Retention exception:** Minimum transaction records required by applicable financial law, anonymised
- **Deadline:** 30 days; third-party processors must be notified in parallel
- **Key articles:** Art. 7, 8, 13-14, 38; third-party processor obligations under Art. 38

### Customer C — Maria (Los Angeles, California)
- **Decision:** Stop-sharing actioned immediately; deletion on hold pending active return dispute
- **Basis for hold:** Rwanda DPL Art. 8 — retention permitted where necessary for contract performance or legal claims
- **Deadline:** Stop-sharing immediate; deletion within 30 days of dispute resolution
- **Key articles:** Art. 7, 8, 13-14

### Comparison Table
All three customers analysed side by side: applicable articles, data retention scope, recommended deadlines, and penalties for non-compliance.

## Rwanda Law No. 058/2021 References
- **Art. 7** — Purpose Limitation / Data Minimisation
- **Art. 8** — Storage Limitation (retention only where legally required)
- **Art. 13–14** — Lawful Basis and Consent (withdrawal triggers cessation of processing)
- **Art. 38** — Controller obligations: technical safeguards, records, DPIA
- **Art. 53** — Penalties: RWF 2,000,000–5,000,000 or 1% of global annual turnover
